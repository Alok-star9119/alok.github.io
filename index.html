Chola Bhatura

Chole Bhatura is a popular Punjabi dish made of spiced chickpea curry (chole) served with deep-fried, puffed flatbreads (bhatura) Note: Use a heavy-bottomed kadhai and hot oil for best puffing.

For Chola Bhatura

Soak 1 cup dried chickpeas overnight. Drain and pressure cook with 2 tea bags, 1 cinnamon stick, 2 cardamom pods, 1 bay leaf, and a pinch of baking soda until soft (4–5 whistles).

Tempering: Heat oil, add cumin seeds, turmeric, and whole spices. Sauté garlic and ginger, then add onions, tomatoes, and cook until golden.

Masala: Add ground spice mix (coriander, cumin, chana masala, red chili, amchur, and kasuri methi), then mix in cooked chickpeas. Simmer 15–20 minutes with tamarind water or reserved chickpea water.

Finish: Add ghee, sliced ginger, and garnish with onions, coriander, and lemon. .

For Bhatura (Fried Flatbreads)

Dough: Mix 1½ cups maida (all-purpose flour), ½ cup semolina, ½ cup curd or yogurt, 1½ tsp baking powder, sugar, salt, and oil. Knead into a soft dough with warm water.

Rest: Cover and let rise for 2–3 hours (or 1 hour with yeast).

Shape & Fry: Roll into oval shapes. Deep-fry in hot oil, pressing with a spoon to puff. Fry until golden brown on both sides

Pasta Aglio e Olio

This traditional Italian pasta dish is the ultimate "pantry meal." It is flavorful, spicy, and incredibly fast to prepare.
Warning: Do not let the garlic burn, or it will turn bitter!

Ingredients

Spaghetti, extra virgin olive oil, 6 cloves of garlic (sliced), red pepper flakes, and parsley.

Procedure

  1. Boil spaghetti in salted water until al dente.
  2. While pasta boils, heat olive oil in a pan and add sliced garlic.
  3. Cook garlic until golden, then add red pepper flakes.
  4. Toss the drained pasta into the oil with a splash of pasta water.
  5. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve.

Fresh Guacamole

The perfect dip for any occasion. Always use ripe Hass avocados for the best texture.

Ingredients

3 avocados, 1 lime (juiced), 1/2 onion (diced), cilantro, and salt.

Procedure

  1. Slice avocados in half, remove pits, and scoop the flesh into a bowl.
  2. Mash the avocado with a fork until desired consistency is reached.
  3. Stir in the lime juice, diced onion, and salt.
  4. Fold in the chopped cilantro gently.
  5. Serve immediately with tortilla chips.

Fluffy Buttermilk Pancakes

Nothing beats a tall stack of pancakes on a Sunday morning. Bonus Tip: Let the batter rest for 10 minutes for extra fluffiness.

Ingredients

Flour, sugar, baking powder, buttermilk, 1 egg, and melted butter.

Procedure

  1. Whisk dry ingredients together in a large bowl.
  2. In another bowl, mix buttermilk, egg, and melted butter.
  3. Combine wet and dry ingredients until just mixed (lumps are okay).
  4. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto a hot greased griddle.
  5. Flip when bubbles appear on the surface and cook until golden brown.

Tomato Basil Bruschetta

A simple appetizer that highlights the freshness of summer tomatoes.
Note: Use a high-quality balsamic glaze for the best finish.

Ingredients

Baguette slices, Roma tomatoes, fresh basil, garlic cloves, and olive oil.

Procedure

  1. Dice tomatoes and mix with chopped basil, olive oil, and salt.
  2. Toast the baguette slices until they are golden and crispy.
  3. Rub a raw garlic clove over the surface of the warm toast.
  4. Spoon the tomato mixture onto the bread.
  5. Drizzle with balsamic glaze and serve immediately.
